# 3 Outreach Email Templates

For landlords and property developers approaching supported-living and social-housing providers directly. Each template has been tested by the providers we work with — they tell us what works.

Fill in the **[bracketed]** details and send. Aim for one provider per email (mass blasts get ignored).

---

## Template 1 — The Direct Forward-Lease Offer

**Use when**: You have a specific property — built or in development — that fits their commissioning brief.

**Subject**: [Council name] supported-living — [4-bed Victorian terrace / new-build extra care] available

---

Hi [Provider contact name — usually Property/Development Director],

I'm [your name], a [landlord / developer] in [council name]. I noticed [provider name] holds the [framework name or contract] with [council], so you're likely looking at portfolio expansion in the area.

I have **[specific property: e.g. a 4-bed Victorian terrace in [postcode area], fully renovated, EICR/EPC C, garden, off-street parking — or — a 12-unit new-build at planning stage, expected handover Q[X] 202X]**.

It fits well for [client group: e.g. mental health step-down / learning disability / 16-25 supported / asylum dispersal]. Happy to forward-lease on a 5–15 year FRI basis at a yield in line with your committee's tolerance — I'll send the rent assumption and HMRC capital allowance position if you want to underwrite quickly.

Would you have 15 minutes this week or next to talk?

Best,
[Your name]
[Phone]
[Website if any]

## Template 2 — The Portfolio Partnership Pitch

**Use when**: You're a landlord with multiple properties or a developer with a pipeline, wanting an ongoing relationship rather than a single deal.

**Subject**: Forward-lease pipeline — [number] units across [borough/county] over [timeframe]

---

Hi [Provider contact name],

I'm [your name] — [brief credibility: e.g. landlord with 12 supported-housing units across the North West / developer working on a 60-unit extra care scheme in [town]].

We're targeting [borough/county] for the next [12-24] months. Looking at our pipeline:

- **Now**: [X units of type Y available immediately]
- **Q[N]**: [X units type Y]
- **Q[N+1]**: [X units type Y]

We'd rather work with one or two operators on a forward-lease basis than chase council void postings. Our property profile is [HMO / mid-terrace / new-build / large family home converted] — broadly aligned with your supported-living / step-down / [client group] brief.

If a recurring conversation makes sense, can we set up a 30-min intro this month? I can share the unit-by-unit specs and rental expectation ahead of the call.

Best,
[Your name]
[Phone]
[Website]

## Template 3 — The Off-Market Building Conversion Pitch

**Use when**: You have an unusual property (ex-care home, large pub, ex-office, decommissioned community building) that needs the right buyer.

**Subject**: [Type of building] in [town/postcode] — direct enquiry, no agent fees

---

Hi [Provider contact name],

I have a [former care home / large period building / ex-office block / Edwardian-villa HMO] in [postcode], [size: e.g. 18 rooms, 4,500 sq ft] that I want to put back into supported-housing use rather than sell on the open market.

Quick details:
- **Location**: [town] — [10-minute walk from town centre / 2 buses to the council offices]
- **Use class**: [C2 / C3 / Sui Generis]
- **Condition**: [refurbished 2023 / needs ~£80k spend / immediately operational]
- **Best fit**: [your guess at client group based on layout — e.g. 16-25 with shared communal, learning disability with garden, MH step-down]
- **Lease terms**: forward-lease 10–25 years, FRI, RPI-linked

I noticed [provider name] holds the [framework / DPS] with [council] — happy to send the floor plans and the [council planning officer / property bid] commentary I already have on file if it's worth a 20-min call.

Best,
[Your name]
[Phone]

## What works (the bit nobody tells you)

1. **One provider, one email, one specific property** — group emails get filtered to junk.
2. **Reference their commissioning contract by name** — proves you've done your homework.
3. **Talk in lease language not asking-price language** — providers don't buy property, they lease it. Forward-lease, FRI, 5–15-year term, RPI uplift.
4. **Reply within 4 hours when they come back** — providers move quickly when their bid pipeline is hot.
5. **Be specific about client group** — "supported living" alone doesn't tell them which of their pathway teams to brief.
